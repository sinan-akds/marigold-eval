Build an event management dashboard called "EventHub".

## Shell

An application shell: a persistent sidebar down the left at full height, a top bar across the top, and a scrollable main content area.

- **Sidebar** — the app name "EventHub" at the top, then a vertical navigation with five entries: Dashboard, Events, Attendees, Reports, Settings. Separate "Settings" from the others with a dividing line. A "Help & Support" entry sits at the bottom. The sidebar can be collapsed and expanded with a toggle.
- **Top bar** — on the left a button to toggle the sidebar; in the center a breadcrumb showing the current page (e.g. "EventHub > Dashboard"); on the right a menu for the user account with "Profile" and "Sign out". Choosing "Sign out" opens a confirmation dialog asking "Are you sure you want to sign out?" with "Cancel" and "Sign out".
- **Main area** — shows the currently selected page; choosing a sidebar entry switches it. The active entry is clearly marked. The default page is Dashboard.

## Dashboard

A heading "Dashboard Overview" and, at the top, an info banner: "Welcome back! You have 3 events starting this week." Below, four summary cards in a row — Total Events (24), Tickets Sold (1,849), Revenue ($45,230), Upcoming (8) — each with a label and a large value; the Revenue card carries a tooltip "Net revenue after fees and refunds". Below the cards, a table "Upcoming Events" with columns Event, Date, Venue, Tickets Sold, Status and at least 6 realistic rows; statuses "On Sale", "Draft", "Sold Out" appear as colored indicators.

## Events

A heading "Events", a search field that filters the table by name or location, and a "Create Event" button. The table has columns Name, Date, Location, Capacity, Status with at least 6 rows. "Create Event" opens a dialog with a form — Event Name (required), Date (date picker), Location, Capacity (numeric), Description (text area) — plus "Create" and "Cancel"; either button closes the dialog.

## Attendees

A heading "Attendees", a search field filtering by name or email, and the attendee count as text (e.g. "6 attendees"). A table with columns Name, Email, Events Attended, Last Active, Status and at least 6 rows; Status is "Active" (success) or "Inactive" (warning) depending on whether the last-active date falls within the last 30 days.

## Reports

A heading "Reports" and tabs for three views:
- **Revenue** — four cards (Total Revenue $45,230, This Month $8,420, Average per Event $1,885, Refunds $1,230) and below them a success banner "Revenue is up 12% compared to last month."
- **Attendance** — four cards (Total Attendees 3,200, Repeat Visitors 890, Average per Event 178, No-shows 145) and a table "Top Events by Attendance" (Event, Date, Attendees, Capacity, Fill Rate, at least 4 rows).
- **Overview** — a summary paragraph and three collapsible sections "Q1 Summary", "Q2 Summary", "Q3 Summary", each with a short paragraph.

## Settings

A heading "Settings" and tabs for two sections:
- **General** — a form with Organization Name, Contact Email (email), Default Currency (USD, EUR, GBP), Default Timezone (UTC, CET, EST, PST) and a "Save Changes" button; saving shows a success banner "Settings saved successfully."
- **Notifications** — four toggles "Email Notifications", "SMS Notifications", "Weekly Digest", "Marketing Emails", each with a one-line description, and a "Save Preferences" button.

## Requirements

- Build everything with Marigold design system components; do not use raw HTML elements.
- Every interactive element must actually work, not just look the part: navigation switches the page, searches filter their tables, dialogs open and close.
- Every table must carry an accessible label.
