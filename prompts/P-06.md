Build a product catalog page for an online merchandise store.

## Header

A page heading "Merchandise Store" and a short description: "Browse our collection of branded merchandise."

## Toolbar

A horizontal bar above the catalog with:
- a search field that filters products by name,
- a control to sort products: Newest, Price: Low to High, Price: High to Low, Most Popular,
- a "Filters" button that opens a filter panel sliding in from the right edge of the screen.

## Filter panel

- **Category** — several selectable options: T-Shirts, Hoodies, Accessories, Posters, Stickers.
- **Price range** — a slider from $0 to $100.
- **Size** — several selectable options: XS, S, M, L, XL.
- **Availability** — a toggle for "In stock only".
- an "Apply Filters" button and a "Reset" button.

## Applied filters

Show the active filters as a row of small removable chips; removing a chip clears that filter. Offer a "Clear all" action while any filter is active. When nothing is filtered, show the muted text "No filters applied".

## Product grid

Show 8 products as cards in a grid: four per row on a wide screen, two on a medium screen, one on a narrow screen. Each card shows the product name, the price (e.g. "$29.99"), a status indicator ("New", "Sale" or "Sold Out"), a one-sentence description, and an "Add to Cart" button. Two of the eight products are sold out and their button is disabled.

The grid must actually react to the search, sort and filter controls.

## Empty state

When the active filters match no products, replace the grid with an empty state: the title "No products found", the text "Try adjusting your filters or search query.", and a "Clear all filters" button.

## Pagination

Below the grid, paging controls showing "Page 1 of 3" with previous and next navigation.

## Requirements

- Build everything with Marigold design system components; do not use raw HTML elements.
- Every interactive element must actually work, not just look the part.
- The layout must adapt responsively to the viewport as described.
